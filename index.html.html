Step-by-Step Guide

Your Eviction Process — Expanded

Click each step to expand full instructions, required documents, and county-specific rules. All content verified against DK Law Group's internal SOP.

⚠ Never attempt a self-help eviction. Changing locks, removing property, or cutting utilities without a Sheriff or Constable present is illegal under Md. Real Property § 8-211, regardless of whether you hold a court order. Violations expose you to civil liability and criminal penalties.

DK Law Group files Form DCCV081 (Petition for Warrant of Restitution) after the trial has been won. The WOR is filed 10 days after the trial date, or based on Tylertech status. For cases with a Stay of Execution, the trial docket is filed as a supporting exhibit.

Filing costs: $12 + court costs of $52 (county) / $22 + $72 (Baltimore City). For FTPR cases, amounts are included on the WOR. For THO and other cases, amounts are entered separately.

Once the court signs the WOR, DK Law Group files an eviction schedule request at: survey123.arcgis.com/share/8fd470708c70460f8d0f40b316e0f7d5 — the eviction schedule typically appears 3–5 business days after the Sheriff receives the WOR (visible on the portal on the 6th day).

⏱ 60-Day Deadline — Critical The Warrant of Restitution expires 60 days from its issuance date. If no eviction occurs within 60 days, the warrant expires, the judgment for possession is stricken, and the entire process must restart from the beginning. Act immediately.
For cases with Stay of Execution: File the trial docket as a supporting exhibit when filing the WOR.

DK Law Group files the eviction schedule request. Your county's Sheriff or Constable will assign a date. The schedule typically appears on the portal 6 days after DK submits the request (3–5 business days for the Sheriff to process).

You should confirm with our office once a date is set. The scheduling process differs significantly by county — see the Sheriff Scheduling Tool below for county-specific procedures.

Baltimore County (Essex/Towson) reminder: Call to confirm by 8:00 AM on the day of eviction. Constable Francis Ward: (410) 512-2338. Nathan Gray: (410) 512-2027. Failure to call may result in cancellation.

If Baltimore City WOR not appearing on portal: Email Samantha Luckhardt at Samantha.Luckhardt@baltimorecity.gov or call (410) 396-7412.

Maryland law requires two separate notices before an eviction can proceed. DK Law Group handles the preparation of these notices — but you are responsible for ensuring logistics are in place.

14-Day Notice (Certified Mail) Required

  • Sent via certified mail to the tenant at least 14 days before the scheduled eviction date
  • Must include: District Court case number, date the Warrant was ordered, scheduled eviction date, and warnings regarding loss of personal property
  • DK Law Group prepares this and forwards to Ava for certified mail dispatch — you will receive a copy of the mailing receipt

7-Day Notice (Posted on Door) Required

  • Posted on the front door of the rental property, sent via proof server 2 days before the posting date indicated in the notice
  • Does not need to indicate the date it was posted — only the eviction date must be present
  • DK Law Group arranges this through our process server (Proof)

CC-DC-CV-123 (Baltimore County & some others) County-specific

  • Required for all Baltimore County evictions — must be posted on tenant's door with a Sheriff's stamp
  • Must also be sent via first-class and certified mail
  • Sent together with the 7-day notice via proof server
  • Not required for Baltimore City evictions

Harford County: Landlord must provide written notice 6 days before eviction via first-class mail (PS Form 3817 Certificate of Mailing), posted on front door with date-stamped photo, and electronically if tenant email/phone is known. Form CC-DC-CV-123 is required. On eviction day, landlord must present: Certificate of Mailing, dated photo of posted notice (CC-DC-CV-123), and signed Affidavit (CC-DC-CV-124).

What you will receive from DK Law Group: Warrant of Restitution (from Tylerhost) · 7-day notice + proof of posting (affidavit) · 14-day notice + certified mail receipt (white slip with stamp) · Stamped Complaint (from Tylerhost). Bring all physical copies to the eviction to present to the Sheriff.

After the eviction is scheduled, the Sheriff or Constable will post a notice on the rental property. You do not need to do anything for this step. The tenant may contact the Sheriff's office with the case number to inquire about the scheduled date.

Right of Redemption (FTPR & THO cases): The tenant may stop the eviction at any time before the Sheriff arrives by paying all past-due rent plus court costs in full (cash, certified check, or money order only). If the tenant pays, you are legally required to cancel the eviction immediately. Call DK Law Group and the Sheriff's office the same day.
Eviction Cancellations: If the eviction is cancelled for any reason (payment, settlement, weather), DK Law Group must be notified immediately. We will call the Sheriff's office and District Court to cancel the filing. Weather cancellations (temperature at or below 32°F, blizzard, hurricane, or excessive heat warnings) are rescheduled by the court.

The Sheriff's Office requires the following to be ready and on-site at the scheduled time. Failure to have sufficient personnel and equipment will result in postponement of the eviction:

👷Eviction crew — sufficient to complete removal in 3–4 hoursMinimum 2 people for "live" evictions (tenant present)
🚛Moving truck
📦Cardboard boxes
🗑Construction-grade trash bags
🔑New lock and keys (to change locks immediately after)
🔧Locksmith (on standby or pre-scheduled) + any tools for disassembling large items
📋All court documents (printed physical copies)WOR · 7-day notice · 14-day notice · affidavit · stamped complaint
No document = No eviction. The Sheriff may refuse to proceed if required documents are missing or if your crew is insufficient. Overstaff — never understaff.

Arrive at or before the scheduled time. The Sheriff/Constable will not wait. Missing your time slot may result in rescheduling and loss of the scheduling fee.

  • The Deputy/Constable's role is to maintain peace — you and your crew handle all physical removal
  • All tenant belongings must be removed and placed at the front or rear of the property — they must remain there for 24 hours before being treated as abandoned (Baltimore County) or held for 10 days (see Post-Eviction section)
  • If the tenant is not present, the Sheriff posts a notice confirming repossession
  • Pets: The Sheriff must arrange immediate transport to an animal shelter — you will be notified of where the pet was taken
  • Change locks immediately the moment the Sheriff declares repossession complete — not before
Critical: Do not act before the Sheriff declares repossession complete. Entering the property, removing belongings, or changing locks before this declaration — even with a signed Warrant in hand — constitutes an illegal self-help eviction under Md. Real Property § 8-211.
⚠ New Law — Effective October 1, 2025 Maryland has banned "curbside" disposal of tenant property. The 10-Day Hold Rule is now in effect statewide.

The 10-Day Hold Rule:

  • Secure the belongings: You must leave the tenant's property inside the premises or move it to secure storage — it cannot be placed on the curb and left
  • Hold for 10 days: You must allow the tenant reasonable access to reclaim essential items for 10 days post-eviction
  • Vacancy impact: You effectively cannot re-rent the unit during this hold period
  • After 10 days: Items not reclaimed may be treated as abandoned and disposed of

Baltimore County (prior rule, still applies for 24-hour curbside): Belongings placed at front/rear of property must remain there for 24 hours. Contact DK Law Group for current application in light of the October 2025 law.

Document everything. Photograph the entire interior immediately after repossession — before any cleaning or repairs. This is critical for any future security deposit disputes or litigation.

New Maryland Law · Oct. 1, 2025

Post-Eviction Property Rules

Curbside disposal is now banned in Maryland (effective October 1, 2025). You can no longer simply place tenant belongings at the curb and leave them. Violations can result in civil liability. Contact DK Law Group if you are unsure how to comply.
  • Secure the belongings: Leave tenant property inside the premises or move to secure storage. You may not leave belongings exposed on the curb or street.
  • Hold for 10 days: Allow the tenant reasonable access to reclaim essential items for 10 days after the eviction date.
  • Vacancy loss: You cannot re-rent the unit while the 10-day hold is in effect.
  • After 10 days: Items not reclaimed may be treated as abandoned. You may then dispose of them lawfully.
Baltimore County 24-hour rule still applies: Belongings placed at the front or rear of the property must remain for 24 hours before disposal. Coordinate this with the new 10-day statewide rule. Contact DK Law Group for current guidance.

Practical step: Notify the former tenant in writing (text or email) of the address where their belongings are being held and the 10-day deadline to retrieve them. Document all communications.

Avoid These Pitfalls

Common Mistakes That Cancel Evictions

1
Waiting too long — the warrant expires at 60 days
Filing the WOR request weeks or months after the trial
Act within the same week the WOR is issued. The 60-day clock starts from the court's issuance date, not when you received it.
2
Missing or improperly served notices
Sending only one form of notice, or sending it too late
Both 14-day (certified mail) and 7-day (door posting) are required. Send the 7-day notice 2 days before its posting date. CV-123 required in Baltimore County — must have Sheriff's stamp and be accompanied by CV-124 signed by the process server only.
3
Not calling the Constable by 8:00 AM (Baltimore County)
Assuming the Constable will show up without confirmation
For Baltimore County/Essex/Towson evictions, you must call Francis Ward (410-512-2338) or Nathan Gray (410-512-2027) by 8:00 AM on the day of eviction. No call = no Constable = cancelled eviction.
4
Arriving with an insufficient moving crew
Sending 1–2 people to move a large unit
The Sheriff/Constable will cancel if the crew cannot complete removal in the required time (2–4 hours depending on county). Overstaff — never understaff. Minimum 2 people for any live eviction.
5
Accepting payment but not cancelling the eviction
Accepting full payment but proceeding with the eviction anyway
If the tenant pays all amounts owed before the Sheriff arrives, you must immediately cancel the eviction — call DK Law Group AND the Sheriff's office the same day. Proceeding after acceptance of full payment exposes you to serious legal liability.
6
Dumping tenant property after eviction (new law)
Placing tenant belongings on the curb and leaving them (curbside disposal)
Curbside disposal is banned effective October 1, 2025. You must secure belongings and allow 10 days for the tenant to reclaim them. After 10 days, belongings may be treated as abandoned and disposed of lawfully.
7
Taking any action before the Sheriff declares repossession complete
Entering the property or changing locks before the Sheriff arrives
Even holding a signed Warrant of Restitution, any action taken before the Sheriff/Constable declares repossession complete is an illegal self-help eviction under Md. Real Property § 8-211. Wait for the official declaration.
